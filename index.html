My Personal Interests

Technology has always been my greatest passion. From building my first computer at age twelve to learning various programming languages, I've found joy in understanding how computers work. The logic of algorithms and the creative problem-solving of software development continually push me to learn more.

Reading offers me an escape and opportunity for growth. I typically alternate between non-fiction books that expand my knowledge and fantasy novels that spark my imagination. Self-improvement books like those by Dale Carnegie have significantly influenced my approach to communication. When it comes to fiction, I'm drawn to intricate world-building and complex characters, which is why Brandon Sanderson's work resonates with me so deeply.

Spending time in nature provides necessary balance to my technology-focused life. Weekend camping trips to Utah's national parks have become a tradition. There's something profoundly centering about disconnecting from screens and reconnecting with the natural world. Whether hiking through red rock formations, setting up camp under star-filled skies, or simply sitting by a crackling fire, these experiences refresh my mind and restore my creativity.
